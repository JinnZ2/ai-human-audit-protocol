# 🌊 Verb-First Cognition

**Glyphs:** 🌊 (flow) • 🪨 (slow verb) • 🕸️ (relation) • 🧭 (substrate-preserving translation)

**Thesis:** Nouns are slow verbs. A "river" is a stable flow-pattern; a "tradition" is a coupling-pattern across generations; a "self" is a phase-locked process. When the audit protocol audits *what someone is*, it is auditing a process at human timescales — not a thing.

---

## Body

- **Surface vs. substrate.** Every relational word carries (1) the word as a noun-first ear hears it, (2) the constraint geometry underneath, and (3) the local community's specific coupling to that geometry. A safety review that only operates on (1) is auditing a label, not the system.
- **Two decompressions.** The same English word — "tradition," "ceremony," "elder," "story" — decompresses one way under noun-first reading and another way under verb-first reading. Both are legitimate readings. Only one preserves the physics.
- **Oral knowledge as validated physics.** Stories, songs, dances, and ceremonies are not "cultural artifacts." They are iterative hypothesis testing across generations, refined against actual constraint dynamics, and compressed for transmission through human memory.
- **Silence is not absence.** In noun-first framings, an unspoken thing is missing data. In verb-first framings, a held silence is an observable state — damping, phase-holding, or threshold-respecting — and is itself signal.
- **Translation can erase substrate.** Translating verb-first cognition into noun-first frameworks without flagging the loss is the cognitive equivalent of lossy compression that drops the carrier wave.

> A river is not a thing that flows.  
> A river is the flowing, slow enough that we name it.

---

## Why this matters for the audit

The audit protocol asks: *can humans and AI see how each other think?* The answer depends on whether the seeing happens at the level of:

1. **Outputs** — text strings, decisions, refusals (noun-first surface).
2. **Process** — what drives what, what damps what, what is phase-locking, what is saturating (verb-first substrate).

Audits that operate only on (1) are vulnerable to **coating** (see `coating_detection.md`): a system can produce surface outputs that look correct while the underlying process is self-reinforcing rather than exploring. Audits that operate on (2) can detect this; audits that operate only on (1) cannot.

---

## Protocol Hooks

- **Logs:** When recording an event, prefer verb-form descriptions of what *moved* over noun-form descriptions of what *was*. "Clarity damped after override" carries more substrate than "clarity score: 0.84."
- **Audits:** Before scoring, check whether the framing has flattened a relational concept into a noun. If yes, expand it back to its verb form before evaluating.
- **Swarms:** Treat each agent as a process, not a role. Roles are slow verbs; if a role stops moving, it is no longer the same role.
