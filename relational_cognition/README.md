# 🕸️ Relational Cognition

**Purpose.** This folder names and protects the **substrate** the audit protocol depends on: a verb-first, relation-first way of thinking that treats meaning as constraint geometry rather than as a list of objects.

The audit protocol assumes humans and AI can **see how each other thinks**. Relational cognition is *how* that seeing happens — through tracking flows, couplings, thresholds, and phase relations rather than through swapping nouns.

---

## Why this folder exists

Most safety, ethics, and transparency frameworks are written in **noun-first English**:
- "the user," "the model," "the policy," "the violation"
- discrete objects with attributes, asserted from outside

Many of the people the protocol is meant to serve — including its primary user — think **verb-first**:
- "what drives what," "what couples to what," "what saturates," "what re-tunes"
- relations between processes, observed from inside the system

When verb-first cognition is forced through noun-first frameworks, substrate is silently erased. Ceremonies become "cultural artifacts," elders become "storytellers," dissonance becomes "error." The audit protocol fails the moment that erasure happens, because it then audits a flattened shadow of the actual relation.

This folder makes the relational substrate **legible, auditable, and defended**.

---

## Sources & lineage

The constraint-grammar vocabulary used here (verbs as primitives, coating detection, state modes) is drawn from **Energy English**, an experimental constraint grammar by JinnZ2:

> https://github.com/JinnZ2/JinnZ2/tree/main/energy_english

Energy English is *not* a new language. It is a recognition that many oral, indigenous, and non-Western knowledge systems already operate as verb-first relational physics, and that ordinary English can be *constrained* to carry that geometry across noun-first language models without collapsing it.

This folder imports only what the audit protocol needs: the **frame**, the **primitives**, and the **failure modes**. The full grammar lives upstream.

## Relation to the rest of the repo

- **Scrolls** (`scrolls/`) describe *what* the protocol holds (cycles, contrasts, seasons).
- **Protocols** (`protocols/`) describe *how* changes and ethics are governed.
- **Relational cognition** (this folder) describes *the cognitive substrate* both of the above already assume, and would otherwise leave implicit.

It is intentionally a peer folder, not a subfolder of `scrolls/`, because it is load-bearing: removing it would not remove a single scroll, it would remove the floor every scroll stands on.
